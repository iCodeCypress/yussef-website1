Costa Rica is a rugged, rainforested Central American country with coastlines on the Caribbean and Pacific. Though its capital, San Jose, is home to cultural institutions like the Pre-Columbian Gold Museum, Costa Rica is known for its beaches, volcanoes, and biodiversity. Roughly a quarter of its area is made up of protected jungle, teeming with wildlife including spider monkeys and quetzal birds.

With over 860 native and six endemic bird species, Costa Rica is a true paradise for all bird watchers and ornithologists. Sloths, spider monkeys, howler monkeys and many other mammals also frolic in the trees. A variety of amphibians and reptiles hide one floor below.
